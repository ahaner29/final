What We Know So Far

Carbon emission from burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas is changing the climate and affecting our planet. The more carbon pollution in the air, the
more the sun's energy is being trapped as heat, which in turn makes the climate hotter. Actually, the world has gotten one degree (in Celsius) warmer since the 1880s.


There are real consequences when it comes to warmer temperatures. Some examples include sea levels rising and extreme weather events. Sea levels all around the world have risen about
20 centimeters since the early 1900s, swallowing entire islands and are getting closer to populated areas like New York, Melbourne, and Dakar. Weather events are also getting more extreme. Hurricanes,
floods, and droughts are becoming more frequent. A common example is Hurricane Harvey; Harvey tore through the Caribbean and Southern United States and destroyed homes and left people without
power for weeks, months in some cases.

Basic Facts

  1. 97% of scientist believe that man-made pollution is causing climate change; only 3% disagree.

  2. Carbon pollutions stays in the atmosphere and traps more of the Sun's heat, making it warmer.

  3. Both carbon and global temperatures have increased dramatically since Industrial Revolution.

  4. Climate change leads to more extreme weather; some areas see intense floods and others
    droughts.

  5. Warmer temperatures cause glaciers to melt, which in turn causes sea levels to rise higher.

  6. The temperature is on pace to rise 4 degrees (Celsius) by 2100. To avoid the awful effects
    of climate change we have to keep the rise below 2 degrees.

  7. The good news is we can get all the energy we need to power technology by switching to
    clean energy without heating the planet.

The following ancient evidence proves that current warming is happening ten times faster than the average rate of ice-age-recovery warming:

  • Global Temperature Rise

    • The planet's average surface temperature has risen about 1.62 degrees
    Fahrenheit since the nineteenth century. This is largely because of the
    increased use of carbon dioxide and other human-made emissions.

  • Warming Oceans

    • The ocean has absorbed much of this increased heat, with the first
    2,300 feet warming up more than 0.4 degrees since 1969.

  • Sea Level Rise

    • Global sea level rose around eight inches in the last century. It
    continues to accelerate slightly every year.

  • Extreme Events

    • The number of record high temperatures in the United States has
    continued to increase, while the number of record number low
    temperatures have been decreasing since the 1950s.

Why am I interested in this topic?

Global warming has already had a significant impact on our communities, health, and climate. Sea levels are rising, the number of extreme events, like
wildfires, are increasing, dangerous heat waves are happening more often, as well as severe droughts (refer to list above). We have to start enacting
change to address climate change before it continues to intensify and affecting the planet even more. There are actually
many practical solutions that we can do that will reduce carbon emission, slow the rate of climate change, and help maintain a healthy, safe world
for future generations. Some of these solutions can be found on the Ways You Can Help tab.
